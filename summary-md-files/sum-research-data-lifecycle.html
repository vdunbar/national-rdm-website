Section Summary

  • The research data life cycle is a model to describe all of the processes and transformations that your data will undergo and be subjected to, beginning from the planning stage of your project, and carrying through its collection, cleaning, analysis, visualization, dissemination, and deposit, and/or archiving.

  • Research data should be considered in terms of its original source, including any biases which shaped its generation, as well as the various versions that it may subsquently take.

  • By keeping track of the evolution of data through version controls, you are creating an audit trail to discern what changed, how it changed, and who changed it, which has many benefits related to RDM.

  • Version control can be accomplished both manually and automatically, both of which have strengths and specific use cases.
